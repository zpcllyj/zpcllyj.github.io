About

I am a postdoctoral research fellow in the Climate Systems Engineering initiative (CSEi) at The University of Chicago, hosted by Prof. Tiffany Shaw and Prof. Da Yang.

Before joining UChicago, I completed my Ph.D. at the Scripps Institution of Oceanography, UC San Diego (2025), and my B.Sc. with Honors in Atmospheric Sciences from the School of Physics, Peking University (2020). I was fortunate to be mentored by Prof. Shang-Ping Xie and Prof. Nicholas Lutsko at Scripps, Prof. Yongyun Hu at PKU, and Prof. Eli Tziperman at Harvard. They, along with many supportive colleagues, taught me valuable knowledge and skills and helped me discover the joy of scientific research.

Research

Tropical circulations, their seasonality, and their links to global climate.

  • 01

    Hadley circulation theory

    Dynamics of the Hadley cell under the combined influence of parameterized eddies and equatorial heating, using axisymmetric and idealized GCM frameworks.

  • 02

    ITCZ asymmetry and seasonal-cycle rectification

    How the seasonal migration of the ITCZ rectifies the annual-mean state, and why interhemispheric asymmetry depends on orbital and forcing configurations.

  • 03

    ENSO and Asian monsoon predictability

    Mechanisms behind the asymmetric monsoon response to El Niño versus La Niña, and identification of non-ENSO precursors of summer monsoon variability.

  • 04

    Seasonal superrotation

    Conditions under which Earth's tropical troposphere transiently superrotates within the annual cycle, and the eddy–mean flow interactions that drive it.

  • 05

    Wind–evaporation–SST feedback

    Role of WES feedback in shaping tropical SST patterns and the coupled response to ENSO and external forcings.
